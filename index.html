Tableau Visualization Analysis

After analyzing the data and creating visualizations, several correlations became apparent.

Bike stations, including the starting and ending locations, located within Jersey City are more popular than bike station locations outside of the city limits. Since the city locations are more popular, bike rentals within the city limits are longer, averaging between 1 to 3 hours, suggesting patrons are finding more things to do and are using the bike for a longer duration. Owners of Citi Bike could increase their business by adding more rental stations within the city limits, to help meet the demand.

The second phenomenon I uncovered directly pertains to patrons’ birth year. After analyzing the data, majority of patrons are around 52 years old, with a birth year of 1969. This suggests older adults are using the bikes more than younger adults or teenagers. This was a surprising find since most adults over 50 are less active than their younger counterparts.

The last phenomenon I found includes the type of patrons who are using the Citi Bike rental stations. Patrons can either be a ‘Customer’ (without a subscription) or a ‘Subscriber’ (with a subscription). Rental stations like Liberty Light Rail has the most ‘customers’ of any station, while Grove St. Path has the most ‘Subscribers’ of any other station. This could be due to income levels fluctuating between districts. If Citi Bike is looking to increase its Subscribers count, a promotion with special subscription pricing would be an excellent way to increase the number of Subscribers, across all locations.
